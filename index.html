Step-by-step guide
1

Open TradingView

Go to tradingview.com and open your pair. Set chart to 1H or 4H.

2

Add your EMAs

Click Indicators → search EMA → add twice. Set one to 20 and one to 50.

3

Mark Support & Resistance

Find recent swing highs (resistance) and swing lows (support). Draw horizontal lines at those levels.

4

Enter values in Signal Tool

Read current price, both EMAs, and S/R levels from your chart. Enter them and click Analyze Setup.

5

Read the signal

Green = Long, Red = Short, Gray = Wait. Use the exact entry, SL, and TP shown. Never override the Wait signal.

6

Manage the trade

Move stop to breakeven at 1:1. Exit at take profit. Always close before major news events.

Entry rules — all must be true
Rule 1 — EMA alignment
20 EMA above 50 EMA for longs. 20 EMA below 50 EMA for shorts. Flat or tangled EMAs = no trade.
Rule 2 — Price location
Longs: price above 20 EMA. Shorts: price below 20 EMA. Never trade against EMA direction.
Rule 3 — S/R confirmation
Longs: price near support. Shorts: price near resistance. This is your entry trigger.
Rule 4 — Minimum 2:1 R:R
TP distance must be at least 2x SL distance. Below 2:1 = skip the trade, no exceptions.
Risk management
Risk 4% per trade
On $400 that's $16 per trade. Never exceed this no matter how confident you feel.
Stop loss placement
5 pips below support (longs) or 5 pips above resistance (shorts). Never widen your stop.
Breakeven rule
Once price moves 1:1 in your favor, move stop to entry. You now have a risk-free trade.
Max 2 trades per day
Quality over quantity. Take your best 1-2 setups only. Stop trading after 2 losses in one day.
When NOT to trade
30 mins before/after NFP, CPI, BOE, Fed
EMAs flat or tangled (sideways market)
R:R below 2:1
Asian session dead hours (8pm-2am EST)
After 2 losing trades — done for the day
